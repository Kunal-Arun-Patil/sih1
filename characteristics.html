🐄 Characteristics of Cattle

1. Body Structure – Generally lighter and smaller compared to buffalo; more angular body.

2. Horns – Usually thin, upright, or curved outward.

3. Skin & Hair – Short hair; skin color varies (white, brown, black, spotted).

4. Ears – Medium-sized, often held upright.

5. Muzzle & Nose – Light-colored, moist nose.

6. Tail – Thinner tail ending with a tuft of hair.

7. Temperament – Comparatively calmer and used for both milk and draft purposes.

🐃 Characteristics of Buffalo

1. Body Structure – Heavier, more compact, and muscular; bulkier than cattle.

2. Horns – Thick, long, and usually curved backward or sideways.

3. Skin & Hair – Darker skin (black/grey); sparse body hair.

4. Ears – Broader and droopy compared to cattle.

5. Muzzle & Nose – Dark-colored, broad, and wet nose.

6. Tail – Heavier and longer, with a bushier end.

7. Temperament – Strong, hardy, and often used for draft power in fields.

🔍 How Breed Recognition Works with These Features

1. Cattle → Recognized by lighter body colors, upright horns, and slimmer build.

2. Buffalo → Identified through dark skin, backward-curving horns, and bulkier frame.
